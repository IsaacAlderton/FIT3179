Global CO2 Emissions and Population Statistics

One of the major contributing factors to climate change is the creation of CO2 emissons from fossil fuels. This visualisation explores trends of CO2 emission production and population growth on a global scale.

Population Density per Country (2023)

The countries with the highest CO2 emissions per capita in 2021 were Australia, Saudi Arabia, Spain, the USA, Mongolia and Oman, all with levels greater than 14.5 tonnes of CO2 per capita. The orthographic projection depicts that the continents of Africa and South America created a relatively low amount of CO2 emissions from fossil fuels per capita when compared to those of North America, Asia, and Oceania.
